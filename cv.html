education and experience

1867-1883

Maria Sklodowska, later known as Marie Curie, was born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland, then part of the Russian Empire. She attended local schools in Poland, where she showed exceptional aptitude in mathematics and science.

1885-1891

At the age of 18, Marie Curie began attending the Floating University, a secret institution that provided education to Polish students when higher education for Poles was prohibited by the Russian authorities. She moved to Paris and enrolled at the Sorbonne (University of Paris), where she studied physics, mathematics, and chemistry.

1893-1894

Marie Curie earned a degree in physics from the Sorbonne, ranking first in her class. She completed a second degree in mathematics, also ranking first in her class.

1894-1896

Marie Curie began her research work in the laboratory of physicist Gabriel Lippmann, focusing on magnetic properties of various steels Marie Curie started her doctoral research on the properties of various types of uranium ores, leading to the discovery of radioactivity.

1898-1911

Marie Curie and Pierre Curie discovered two new elements, polonium and radium, marking the birth of the field of nuclear physics. The Curies, along with Henri Becquerel, were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for their groundbreaking work on radioactivity. Marie Curie received a second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for her discovery of radium and polonium, and her isolation of radium.
